Steve Jobs

Visionary • Innovator • Co-Founder of Apple Inc.

About Steve Jobs

Steven Paul Jobs (February 24, 1955 – October 5, 2011) was an American entrepreneur, inventor, and business magnate. He was the co-founder, chairman, and CEO of Apple Inc. Jobs was widely recognized as a pioneer of the personal computer revolution and a master of product design and marketing.

Early Life

Steve Jobs was born in San Francisco, California, and was adopted shortly after birth. He grew up in Silicon Valley, where his interest in electronics and innovation began at an early age. He later attended Reed College but dropped out to pursue his passion for creativity and technology.

Career Journey

In 1976, Steve Jobs co-founded Apple with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. After being ousted from Apple in 1985, he founded NeXT and acquired Pixar, which later became a major animation studio. In 1997, Jobs returned to Apple and led the company to historic success.

Major Achievements

  • Co-founded Apple Inc. (1976)
  • Introduced the Macintosh (1984)
  • Revolutionized music with the iPod & iTunes
  • Transformed smartphones with the iPhone
  • Led the creation of the iPad
  • Turned Pixar into a world-leading animation studio

Legacy

Steve Jobs changed the way the world uses technology. His focus on simplicity, user experience, and design excellence continues to influence modern technology, startups, and creative industries. His famous words — "Stay hungry, stay foolish" — remain an inspiration to millions.
